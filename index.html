Guilford College

Local

Guilford College is a small liberal arts college with a strong sense of community and a commitment to sustainability and social justice.
Founded in 1837 by members of the Religious Society of Friends (Quakers), Guilford College has a rich history and tradition of fostering critical thinking, ethical leadership, and inclusive excellence.
With a student-to-faculty ratio of 12:1, Guilford offers personalized attention and a supportive learning environment where students can thrive academically and personally. Guilford's beautiful 340-acre campus features a mix of historic and modern buildings, wooded areas, and open green spaces, providing an inspiring setting for learning and growth.

Academics

Guilford College offers a wide range of academic programs, including more than 40 majors and minors in the arts, humanities, sciences, and social sciences.
Students can also design their own major or minor through Guilford's innovative interdisciplinary studies program.
Guilford's faculty are dedicated teachers and scholars who are committed to student success and engagement.

Student Life

Guilford College offers a vibrant and diverse student life experience, with a wide range of clubs, organizations, and activities for students to get involved in.
From student government to intramural sports, there are plenty of opportunities for students to connect with their peers and make a difference on campus.
Guilford's campus is also home to a number of cultural and social events, including concerts, lectures, and art exhibits, providing students with a rich and dynamic college experience.
